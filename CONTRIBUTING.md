**This is the most important part.** Before we can accept _any_ code from you, you **must** sign our Contributor License Agreement (CLA).

- **Why?** The CLA is a legal document that gives us (Gordon & Teixeira Lda.) the necessary rights to incorporate your work into our main product. It grants us a license to use your contribution in both our source-available version and our commercial SaaS. This is what allows us to run our business while building a great community. It also confirms that you are the original author of the work.

- **How?** It's easy! When you open your first Pull Request, a bot (CLA Assistant) will automatically post a comment with a link to sign the agreement. You only need to do this once.

We cannot legally merge a pull request from any user who has not signed the CLA.

## üöÄ How to Contribute

We welcome contributions of all kinds, from bug reports to new features.

### üêû Reporting Bugs

- Please check the [Issues](https://github.com/djinilabs/timeclout/issues) page to see if the bug has already been reported.
- If not, open a new issue. Be sure to include:
  - A clear and descriptive title.
  - Steps to reproduce the bug.
  - What you expected to happen.
  - What _actually_ happened (including error messages or screenshots).

### üí° Suggesting Features

- Open a new issue to discuss your idea. This lets us coordinate and make sure it aligns with the project's goals before you spend a lot of time on it.
- Clearly explain the problem your feature would solve and how it would work.

### Pull Request Workflow

1.  **Fork** the repository to your own GitHub account.
2.  **Clone** your fork to your local machine: `git clone https://github.com/djinilabs/timeclout.git`
3.  **Create a new branch** for your changes: `git checkout -b feature/my-amazing-feature`
4.  **Make your changes.** Please follow the coding style of the project.
5.  **Commit** your changes with a clear message: `git commit -m "feat: Add amazing feature"`
6.  **Push** your branch to your fork: `git push origin feature/my-amazing-feature`
7.  **Open a Pull Request (PR)** from your fork's branch to the `main` branch of the official TimeClout repository.
8.  **Sign the CLA** when the bot prompts you (one-time only).
9.  We will review your code, provide feedback, and merge it when it's ready.
